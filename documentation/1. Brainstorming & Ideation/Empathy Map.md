## Persona

**Ravi, 38 — smallholder farmer.** Owns 3 acres. Has a recent soil-test report and a rough sense
of local rainfall. Comfortable using a smartphone browser but not a data expert.

## Empathy Map

### 🗣️ Says
- "I have my soil report, but which crop is actually right for it?"
- "Last year's crop did badly — I don't want to repeat that mistake."
- "Just tell me clearly what to plant."

### 🧠 Thinks
- "Am I wasting fertiliser and water on the wrong crop?"
- "The extension officer is too busy to look at my numbers."
- "Can I trust an app's recommendation?"

### 🙌 Does
- Talks to neighbours and follows whatever they plant.
- Keeps the soil-test slip but rarely acts on the exact numbers.
- Searches online but finds only generic advice.

### ❤️ Feels
- **Anxious** — a bad crop choice risks a whole season's income.
- **Overwhelmed** — too many crop options, unclear trade-offs.
- **Hopeful** — willing to try a simple tool that gives a clear answer.

### 😣 Pains
- No easy way to map soil + climate numbers to a specific crop.
- Expert advice is slow, scarce and inconsistent.
- Generic web content doesn't account for *his* plot's readings.

### 🎯 Gains
- A fast, specific recommendation with a confidence level.
- Alternative crop options to compare.
- Plain-language agronomic tips (season, pH, water need) he can act on.
- A saved history to track and compare past decisions.
